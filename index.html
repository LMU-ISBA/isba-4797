Course Description

This course presents a student with a challenge and an opportunity to build a portfolio-worthy project to solve a real business problem by integrating business and technical knowledge and skills. For employers, it represents a clear snapshot of a student's understanding of the subject matter and their ability to identify a meaningful project and exercise initiative. It also presents students with first-hand experience to develop project management, teamwork, and communication skills critical for an IT career. A project proposal and instructor consent are required.

Prerequisites: ISBA 3710; ISBA 3730 or CMSI 185; BCOR 3750, all with a grade of C- (1.7) or higher. ISBA 3720 or ISBA 4796 with an approved Capstone Project proposal.

University Core fulfilled: Flags: Oral Skills, Writing.

Course Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, students are expected to be able to:

  1. Develop a formal requirements document to define a proposed system's business value, functionality, and implementation plan.
  2. Apply an agile project management methodology to organize and manage resources to complete a project.
  3. Develop and refine oral, written, and visual communication to productively engage a project's stakeholders.
  4. Learn to acquire new technical skills to fulfill a project's deliverables while integrating pre-existing technical knowledge.

CBA Core Learning Objectives

  • Proficient in choosing appropriate methodologies, models, and techniques to develop estimates and project management plans for information systems projects

Capstone Project and Sprint Rubric

Sprints 01 – 04 integrate business application, skill acquisition, and project management as criteria for the Sprint's grade.

Business Application

Creating value for the project owner/stakeholder is the project's key goal. The students should fulfill the requirements for a viable product and go beyond and above to ensure the output creates immediate and practical business value. The final product should demonstrate a deep and comprehensive understanding of the problems and provide an efficient, effective, and understandable solution to the problems. The product is expected to offer a reliable and practical solution in both technical and business aspects. The code needs to run bug-free, resilient to technical difficulties, and ready to be deployed. The product must come with the necessary documentation for deployment and further maintenance.

Skill Acquisition

Learning and applying new knowledge and skills required by the project is essential for successful project completion. Each student is expected to identify, in consultation with the capstone advisor, and complete a minimum of 16 hours of self-learning modules and apply that in the project.

Project Management

Proper project management has proved to be a critical success factor in any project. Students are expected to follow Scrum as our project management framework and use Jira and Confluence applications to consistently plan, report, and review their progress during the project. As per the tentative schedule, student teams will plan, deliver, and review 4 Sprints (working products) throughout the semester before the final presentation.

Presentations

Each team will make four official presentations of their project output as part of the Sprint Review. The presentations address the problem, its importance and potential impact, the tools and methods, the products, and business implications. Students are expected to rehearse for a smooth and professional presentation focusing on connecting and engaging their audience. Business attire is required for the final presentation. Attendance for the entire 45-minute presentation session is mandatory for the teams presenting in the session. The other capstone advisors, IBSA faculty, and external stakeholders will be present at the presentation session.

Written Report

The final product is expected to come with a professional report that details the problem, its importance, the tools and methods used, and the resulting product. The report should use evidence-based arguments to discuss the technical and business implications of the product and any future work that could follow. The written report is delivered as a GitHub README file.

Professionalism

You are expected to take responsibility for your work, communicate effectively, collaborate respectfully, and demonstrate reliability and accountability in all interactions. I will treat you like a direct report.
