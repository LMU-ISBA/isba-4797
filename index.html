Course Description

This course presents a student with a challenge and an opportunity to build a portfolio-worthy project to solve a real business problem by integrating business and technical knowledge and skills. For employers, it represents a clear snapshot of a student's understanding of the subject matter and their ability to identify a meaningful project and exercise initiative. It also presents students with first-hand experience to develop project management, teamwork, and communication skills critical for a career in the Information Systems & Business Analytics field. A project proposal and instructor consent are required.

Prerequisites: ISBA 3710; ISBA 3730 or CMSI 185; BCOR 3750, all with a grade of C- (1.7) or higher. ISBA 3720 or ISBA 4796 with an approved Capstone Project proposal.

Course Outcomes

Upon completion of this course, students are expected to be able to:

  1. Develop a formal requirements document to define a proposed system's business value, functionality, and implementation plan.
  2. Apply an agile project management methodology to organize and manage resources to complete a project.
  3. Develop and refine oral, written, and visual communication to productively engage a project's stakeholders.
  4. Learn to acquire new technical skills to fulfill a project's deliverables while integrating pre-existing technical knowledge.

CBA Core Learning Objectives

  • Demonstrate an understanding of the core concepts in information systems
  • Proficient in choosing appropriate methodologies, models, and techniques to develop estimates and project management plans for information systems projects
  • Accurately utilize methodologies, tools, and models to transform business requirements into operational information systems

35 Jira Sprint Backlog 3 - Excellent:
• Sprint backlog contains a sufficient number of issues to fully utilize team capacity for a 2-week sprint.
• Sprint is started on time with no delays.
• All issues are clearly prioritized in alignment with sprint goals and team commitments.
• All issues are fully detailed, including:
  - Title
  - Description
  - Assignee
  - Story point estimate
  - Evidence of progress and completion (comments, links, attachments)
  - Relevant documentation or links
• Team members demonstrate active participation in backlog grooming, ensuring issues are well-defined and understood by all.
2 - Fair:
• Sprint backlog includes a reasonable number of issues but does not fully utilize team capacity for a 2-week sprint.
• Sprint is delayed by up to 1 day due to issues lacking sufficient detail or planning.
• Some issues are prioritized, but there are gaps or inconsistencies in aligning priorities with sprint goals.
• Most issues are sufficiently detailed, but some are missing minor elements like subtasks, comments, or full descriptions.
• Limited team involvement in backlog grooming, leading to minor misunderstandings or inefficiencies.
1 - Poor:
• Sprint backlog significantly underestimates the number of issues needed to meet the team's capacity for a 2-week sprint.
• Sprint is started more than 1 day late due to insufficient preparation.
• Issues are not prioritized or lack clear alignment with sprint objectives.
• Many issues are incomplete or missing critical details, such as:
  - Complete description
  - Story point estimate
  - Evidence of progress and completion (comments, links, attachments)
  - Relevant documentation or links
• Minimal or no team involvement in backlog grooming, resulting in unclear or undefined issues that block progress.
10 GitHub Code and README Updates 3 - Excellent:
• Commits are made regularly and align with project milestones, showing clear incremental progress.
• Commit messages are descriptive, meaningful, and follow established format (e.g., "Fix bug in user authentication flow").
• README is comprehensive, up-to-date, and includes the required sections.
• README effectively communicates the project's purpose and status to both technical and non-technical audiences.
2 - Fair:
• Commits are made inconsistently, with periods of inactivity or commits that do not reflect steady progress.
• Commit messages are present but often vague or lacking detail (e.g., "Update code" or "Fix bug").
• README is mostly complete but missing some recent updates in key areas such as installation instructions or installation instructions).
• README provides a general understanding of the project but lacks clarity or detail in some areas.
1 - Poor:
• Commits are rare, with significant gaps in version control activity.
• Commit messages are unclear, uninformative, or missing altogether.
• README is outdated, incomplete, or poorly organized, making it difficult to understand the project's purpose or use.
• Key details are missing from the README, such as setup instructions, project description, or relevant links, hindering collaboration or usability.
40 Sprint Review Presentation 3 - Excellent:
• Demonstrates significant incremental progress with a working product feature that fully aligns with the sprint goal.
• The feature operates as intended, with no critical bugs or errors.
• Team effectively communicates how the feature contributes to the overall product, using clear and professional demos.
• Feedback from stakeholders is solicited and incorporated into future plans.
2 - Fair:
• Demonstrates incremental progress with a partially functional feature, but with some direction is clear.
• Some aspects of the feature require workarounds or temporary fixes, but the overall direction is clear.
• Team explains the current limitations and outlines a realistic plan to address them.
• Stakeholders are engaged but provide limited or mixed feedback on progress.
1 - Poor:
• Unable to demonstrate meaningful progress toward the sprint goal due to incomplete or non-functional product features.
• Major blockers or bugs prevent the feature from being useful or operational.
• Team struggles to clearly articulate challenges, and plans to resolve issues are vague or undefined.
• Stakeholder feedback highlights significant gaps in progress or understanding of priorities.
5 Sprint Retrospective 3 - Excellent:
• Thoughtful insights are shared, reflecting deep reflection on team performance and the sprint process.
• Actionable improvements are proposed, with specific methods to address challenges or enhance future sprints.
• Celebrates successes and acknowledges individual or team achievements.
2 - Fair:
• Provides general feedback with some relevance to team performance and the sprint process.
• Suggestions for improvement are limited or lack actionable detail.
1 - Poor:
• Feedback is vague, unrelated to the sprint, or non-existent.
• No actionable suggestions for improvement are provided, or suggestions are impractical.
• Successes and achievements are ignored or undervalued.
100 TOTAL
